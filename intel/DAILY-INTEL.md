### 1. Trump Administration Blacklists Anthropic from Government Work
**Source:** [Reuters](https://www.reuters.com/world/us/trump-says-he-is-directing-federal-agencies-cease-use-anthropic-technology-2026-02-27/) | [Axios](https://www.axios.com/2026/02/27/anthropic-pentagon-supply-chain-risk-claude) | [NYT](https://www.nytimes.com/2026/02/27/technology/openai-agreement-pentagon-ai.html)

Defense Secretary Pete Hegseth designated Anthropic a "supply-chain risk to national security," cutting the company off from all US government business. The move follows months of talks breaking down over whether Anthropic's safety policies could constrain military action.

**Why it matters:** Most consequential AI policy decision to date. Anthropic's safety-first stance (constitutional AI, refusals) collided with defense use cases. OpenAI immediately stepped in with Pentagon agreement.

**Source:** [Hacker News #1](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47196582) | [rockoder.com](https://www.rockoder.com/beyondthecode/cognitive-debt-when-velocity-exceeds-comprehension/)
